## Research Overview

Research in the Pergande Lab aims to uncover molecular mechanisms driving neurodegenerative diseases, with a focus on the contribution of glial cells — astrocytes, oligodendrocytes, and microglia — to disease onset and progression.

We use high-resolution, mass spectrometry-based multiomic approaches, including proteomics, lipidomics, and metabolomics, to define cell-type-specific dysfunction and identify novel therapeutic targets.

## Current Focus

Ongoing projects include multiomic profiling of Giant Axonal Neuropathy and related aggregate disorders, mechanistic dissection of glial contributions to neurodegeneration, and identification of glia-specific molecular changes that may inform therapeutic development.
